## 1. Archives written as prose, not as a `;`-separated list

The Author Instructions describe Archives as a semicolon-separated list of archive locations, but many bios are written as a prose paragraph (sometimes with intra-sentence semicolons). The parser splits on `; ` and the entry page renders multi-item sections as a `<ul>` — so prose entries appear as bullet points that don't make sense.

**Current rule:** 1 item ⇒ `<p>`, 2+ items ⇒ `<ul>`. Entries below have 2+ items but average item length > 150 chars, which strongly suggests prose rather than list.

Entries to review:

- `meyer-ei-2015` — 3 items, avg 326 chars
- `crowdy-re-2014` — 3 items, avg 255 chars
- `chisholm-b-2014` — 2 items, avg 251 chars
- `salim-sa-2016` — 2 items, avg 246 chars
- `davies-da-2016` — 3 items, avg 203 chars

- `bonnet-h-2024` — 2 items, avg 173 chars
- `rooth-i-2025` — 2 items, avg 172 chars

- `myrdal-g-2014` — 2 items, avg 151 chars

**Possible mitigations:**
- Edit the docx (in `bios-source/`) to either (a) write a coherent prose paragraph without internal `;`, or (b) restructure as actual `;`-separated archive locations.
- Or extend the parser to detect prose (e.g. avg item length > 150 chars ⇒ render as `<p>` rather than `<ul>`).

## 2. Multi-role entries — residuals after the 2026-05-18 fixes

Wyndham-White, Phelan, Rey were fixed by edits in `bios-transforms.ts` and now parse with full roles/orgs.

Two residuals remain that were *not* worth a cosmetic edit:

- **`eichhoff-jj-2024`** — second "role" extracted is the literal fragment `"charged with preparing the regulatory framework"`. The author's docx phrases his 1815–1819 work as that long descriptive clause rather than as a titled office, and the parser picks it up because it ends in a year span. Leaving as-is; rewriting would editorialise.
- **`holtrop-mw-2021`** — first-role title parses as `"President and Chairman"`. The author wrote this as a single combined office (`"President and Chairman of the Board of Directors of the BIS"`). The parser is treating it correctly; if you'd prefer it split into two roles, the docx would need to actually present them as two.

## 3. Organisation abbreviation not captured when the parenthesised text is mid-string

- **`black-er-2014`** — role text is `"third President of the International Bank for Reconstruction and Development (IBRD) or World Bank (since 1956 World Bank Group) 1949-1962"`. The parser's `(ABBR)` regex only matches a parenthesised abbreviation at the end of the org string; `(since 1956 World Bank Group)` doesn't fit either pattern, so the abbreviation falls back to none. Functionally the row shows the org's full text in the listing — no data loss, just not as compact as other entries.

Possible mitigations:
- Add a parser-side step that prefers the *first* parenthesised `[A-Z]+` token in the org text as the abbreviation.
- Or shorten the org wording in the docx (lossy).

## 4. URLs with embedded `;` (fixed; logged for posterity)

`Carrington-PAR 2016.docx` had a URL like `http://archives.nato.int/;search?query=Carrington`. RFC 3986 permits `;` in URL paths, but the parser used to split on it, fragmenting one citation into two. Fixed by tightening the split to `;` followed by whitespace.
